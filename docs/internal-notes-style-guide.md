# Internal Notes Style Guide

Last updated: 17 June 2026

Purpose: keep `/admin/leads` internal notes useful, factual and safe.

## Good Notes

Use short factual notes:

```text
17 Jun: Called customer. They have one quote and apartment access concerns. Suggested quote review before site measure.
```

```text
17 Jun: Missing appliance list and benchtop details. Ask customer to send quote pages that show inclusions/exclusions.
```

```text
17 Jun: Controlled tester. Feedback: request-review form was clear on mobile, but they expected a faster explanation of PC sums.
```

## Note Structure

Prefer:

- date
- contact action
- customer need
- missing information
- next safe step

## Avoid

Do not write:

- supplier costs
- internal rates
- margin or markup
- hidden pricing logic
- lead score
- admin priority
- private credentials
- emotional judgement
- legal conclusions
- compliance conclusions
- HBC approval conclusions
- strata approval conclusions

## Safe Next-Step Language

Use:

- `needs quote review`
- `needs written clarification`
- `may require site measure`
- `may require strata/access review`
- `licensed trade review may be required`
- `written scope confirmation required`

Avoid:

- `approved`
- `compliant`
- `certified`
- `guaranteed`
- `final price`
- `legal advice given`

## Retention

Internal notes are operational records. Do not use notes as a private CRM for unrelated personal details.
