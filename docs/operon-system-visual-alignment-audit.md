# Operon System Visual Alignment Audit

Last updated: 28 June 2026

Deployment status: not deployed.

## Purpose

This local-only audit records how Operon Kitchens is being aligned to the public Operon Flooring visual standard while preserving the Kitchens quote-clarity, quote-review and site-measure journey.

Operon Flooring was used only as a public visual reference for brand rhythm. No Operon Flooring source files, assets, production settings or repository files were opened or modified.

## Public Flooring Reference Notes

- Header: compact white header, restrained logo scale, calm navigation spacing and one clear quote-first CTA.
- Hero: small eyebrow, strong headline, concise subcopy, primary/secondary CTAs and a proof-oriented support panel rather than a dense dashboard.
- Cards: warm white panels, restrained borders, low visual noise and consistent spacing.
- Buttons: deep navy primary action with confident but quiet contrast; secondary actions remain clear without competing.
- Footer: compact dark rounded footer treatment, organised link columns, restrained logo scale and clear legal/copyright copy.
- Mobile rhythm: stacked content, controlled CTA height, no horizontal overflow and compact floating assistance.

## Kitchens Differences Found

- Kitchens has a more complex customer journey than Flooring because it combines planning estimate, quote review, request review and site-measure preparation.
- Kitchens needs stronger safety language around planning estimates, written scope confirmation, licensed trade review and not legal advice.
- The public shell was already close locally, but needed a current audit note, explicit Operon System palette aliases and tighter footer/company link coverage.
- The chatbot already had safe copy and separated prompts, but needed a slightly more branded visual treatment so it felt less like a generic widget.

## Alignment Applied Locally

- Kept the compact white header and branch identity treatment: `OPERON | KITCHENS` through the supplied Kitchens logo assets.
- Preserved quote-first navigation: estimate, quote review, how it works, site measure, guides and request review.
- Added explicit Operon System palette variables for navy, gold, warm white, charcoal, border, muted copy, cards and soft shadow.
- Kept the homepage hero focused on estimate plus quote review, with the sample range as support rather than the dominant message.
- Kept footer compact and grouped links into Quote & review, Services, Guides and Areas & company while keeping `/admin/leads` out of public navigation.
- Added `/pc-sums-provisional-sums` as a short alias to the canonical PC sums guide rather than creating a separate duplicate SEO page.
- Tightened chatbot visual styling with navy/gold treatment, warm border/shadow and separated prompt buttons.

## Kitchens-Specific Choices Preserved

- Planning estimate and quote review remain the main public conversion paths.
- Site measure and written scope confirmation remain required before contract pricing.
- No final quote, legal approval, compliance approval, guaranteed savings, payment, full CRM or public advanced-tool exposure was added.
- `/design-brief` and `/scope-builder` stay hidden and feature-flagged.
