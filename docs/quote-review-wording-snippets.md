# Quote Review Wording Snippets

Last updated: 23 June 2026

Purpose: provide customer-safe wording snippets for manual quote-review trials.

Deployment status: not needed. These snippets are local operator support only.

## Scope Clarity

```text
The quote gives a useful starting point, but several scope items should be confirmed in writing before comparing the total with another provider.
```

```text
Cabinetry appears to be described, but demolition, rubbish removal and trade relocation need clearer inclusion/exclusion wording.
```

## Allowance Risk

```text
Allowance items are not automatically a problem, but they should be specific enough that you understand what is included and what could change.
```

```text
Before relying on the total, ask whether appliances, splashback and benchtop cut-outs are fixed inclusions, PC sums or provisional allowances.
```

## Missing Information

```text
Photos, rough measurements and an appliance list would improve review confidence. Without these, the review should treat service relocation and access assumptions as needing confirmation.
```

## Site Measure

```text
The safest next step is to confirm site conditions, dimensions, access and services through a site measure before moving toward project-specific pricing.
```

## Service Relocation

Use these when a customer mentions moving the sink, cooktop, oven, dishwasher, power points, lighting, rangehood or gas line. Keep wording practical and avoid presenting the review as trade inspection.

```text
Service relocation may require confirmation before quote totals can be compared. Ask whether the quote includes the current and proposed service locations, licensed trade attendance and any provisional allowances for plumbing, electrical or gas work.
```

```text
Moving services can affect trade sequencing, site access and make-good work. The quote should state whether plumbing, electrical or gas changes are included, excluded or provisional before the customer relies on the total.
```

## Plumbing

```text
If the sink, dishwasher or water/waste location is changing, ask whether licensed plumbing work is included and whether reconnection, waste changes and site access are allowed for.
```

```text
Plumbing relocation is a review item, not an automatic problem. It should be clear whether it is fixed in the quote, excluded, or treated as a provisional item subject to site review.
```

## Electrical

```text
If the cooktop, oven, power points, rangehood or lighting locations are changing, ask whether licensed electrical work is included and whether switchboard/load assumptions need review.
```

```text
Electrical relocation should be confirmed in writing before commitment. The review should not treat it as included unless the quote clearly says so.
```

## Gas

```text
If gas is involved, ask whether licensed gas disconnection, reconnection or relocation is included, excluded or provisional. Gas work should be confirmed by an appropriately licensed trade.
```

```text
Gas scope should not be assumed from a kitchen renovation total. It may require project-specific confirmation before site measure and written scope confirmation.
```

## Make-Good After Demolition

```text
Demolition can leave wall, floor, plastering, painting or tile-repair work that is easy to miss. Ask whether make-good items are included, excluded or by others.
```

```text
Before comparing totals, confirm whether the quote includes patching, plastering, tile removal repair, flooring make-good and painting after the existing kitchen is removed.
```

## Older-Property Risk

```text
For older properties, demolition and service work may require extra review for asbestos or hazardous materials. This is a general review prompt only and is not legal advice or building certification.
```

```text
If the property is older or the existing kitchen has old linings, tiles or unknown materials, ask what review is required before demolition starts.
```

## Compliance/Risk Prompt

```text
These are general review prompts only. They are not legal advice, compliance approval, HBC approval, strata approval or building certification. Project-specific confirmation is required.
```

## Written Scope

```text
Ask for written scope confirmation before commitment so inclusions, exclusions, allowances and site-measure assumptions are clear.
```

## Do Not Use

- final price
- guaranteed saving
- quote approved
- legally checked
- compliant
- certified
- HBC approved
- strata approved
- final quote
- final price
- order instantly
