The likely review risk is not just product selection; it is whether service relocation, demolition, trade scope, older-property conditions and site-measure assumptions are clearly included before the customer compares totals.

## Review Readiness

Label: `Basic review ready`

Reason:

- A written quote exists.
- The customer has photos and rough measurements.
- The message clearly identifies service movement and demolition questions.
- The report cannot become `Strong review ready` until there is a measured layout, appliance schedule, service-location notes, switchboard/gas/plumbing context and clearer line-item quote detail.

What would improve confidence:

- measured kitchen plan with current and proposed sink/cooktop positions
- appliance schedule, especially cooktop type
- quote pages showing inclusions, exclusions, PC sums and provisional sums
- photos of services, switchboard location, plumbing access and floor/wall conditions
- notes on age of property and known asbestos or hazardous-material history

## Scope Clarity

What appears included:

- cabinetry replacement is likely included
- benchtop replacement is likely included
- installation is likely included
- some finish selections may be allowed for

What appears unclear:

- demolition of existing cabinetry, tiles and benchtop
- rubbish removal and disposal
- plumbing relocation for sink and dishwasher
- electrical relocation for cooktop, oven, power points and lighting
- gas disconnection or reconnection if a gas cooktop is involved
- plastering, patching, flooring make-good and wall repair after demolition
- splashback supply and installation
- appliance supply, owner-supplied appliances or PC sums
- older-property/asbestos review before demolition
- final site measure and trade inspection assumptions

Written clarification needed:

Ask the provider to confirm whether service relocation and demolition are fixed inclusions, exclusions or provisional items. Do not compare totals until these items are clarified.

## Allowance Risk

Allowance risk label: `High`

Reason:

- service relocation can materially affect scope and trade sequencing
- older-property demolition may reveal conditions that require review
- appliance, benchtop, splashback and make-good details are not clear
- provisional sums may hide trade or site-condition risk

PC sums:

- Ask whether appliances, sink, tapware, handles, splashback tiles or fixtures are fixed selections, PC sums or excluded items.

Provisional sums:

- Ask whether plumbing relocation, electrical relocation, gas work, demolition, rubbish removal, patching or access conditions are provisional sums.

Trade/access allowance:

- Ask whether the quote includes licensed trade attendance, disconnection/reconnection, testing, access constraints and return visits.

## Missing Information

Missing or unclear:

- measured existing layout and proposed layout
- appliance list and cooktop fuel type
- current and proposed service locations
- electrical load/switchboard context
- plumbing access and waste location
- gas disconnection/reconnection requirements
- demolition scope and rubbish removal
- wall/floor make-good after tile and cabinetry removal
- older-property/asbestos or hazardous-material review
- benchtop material, joins, cut-outs and edge detail
- splashback scope
- site measure and licensed trade review timing

## General Compliance And Risk Prompts

These are general review prompts only and are not legal advice, compliance approval or building certification.

- Written contract review may be required for residential building work over $5,000 including GST.
- Deposit guidance should be reviewed, including 10% maximum deposit guidance.
- HBC review may be required for projects over $20,000 including GST.
- Licensed plumbing, electrical and gas trades require review where those works are included.
- Service relocation may require project-specific trade inspection before pricing commitment.
- Engineered-stone restrictions may affect benchtop or splashback options.
- Older properties may require asbestos or hazardous-material review before demolition.
- Final site measure is required before project-specific pricing and written scope confirmation.
