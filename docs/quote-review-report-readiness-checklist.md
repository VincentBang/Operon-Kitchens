# Quote Review Report Readiness Checklist

Last updated: 2 June 2026

Purpose: prepare manual quote-review report trials before building paid review, PDF export, file downloads, payment or CRM workflows.

Default rule: local planning only. Do not launch paid quote review or live report delivery without Vincent approval.

## Minimum Inputs For A Useful Manual Review

Ask for:

- customer name and suburb
- property type
- current project stage
- existing quote if available
- photos or plans if available
- appliance list if available
- rough layout or measurements
- known strata/access constraints
- customer's main concern

If documents are missing, the report can still be prepared, but it should use a lower readiness label and clearer assumptions.

## Readiness Labels

Use one of:

- `Needs more detail`
- `Basic review ready`
- `Strong review ready`

Avoid using a harsh primary score such as `0/100`.

## Required Customer Sections

Every report draft should include:

- scope clarity
- allowance risk
- missing information
- unclear items
- customer questions
- compliance and risk prompts
- recommended next step
- site measure and written scope reminder

## Customer-Safe Language Gate

Before sharing a report, confirm it does not say or imply:

- final fixed quote
- guaranteed savings
- legal advice
- compliance approval
- HBC approval
- strata approval
- certified or compliant outcome
- another contractor's quote is approved or rejected

Use:

- general guidance only
- not legal advice
- not a final quote
- requires review
- may require confirmation
- subject to site measure
- written scope confirmation required

## Internal Field Gate

Do not include:

- supplier costs
- internal rates
- margin or markup logic
- hidden pricing logic
- lead score
- admin priority
- internal notes
- service keys or credentials

## Manual Trial Workflow

1. Select a controlled-test lead from `/admin/leads`.
2. Copy only customer-safe details into the report draft.
3. Mark readiness as `Needs more detail`, `Basic review ready` or `Strong review ready`.
4. Complete each required customer section.
5. List questions the customer can ask before comparing totals.
6. Add the compliance/risk prompt disclaimer.
7. Add the recommended next step.
8. Save trial notes internally; do not send as a paid product until approved.

## When To Recommend Site Measure

Recommend site measure when:

- measurements are missing or unreliable
- service relocation is unclear
- apartment/strata access needs review
- older-property demolition risk exists
- benchtop/splashback details are unclear
- the customer appears close to commitment
- written scope cannot be clarified from the supplied documents

## Deferred

Do not build yet without explicit approval:

- paid report checkout
- PDF generation
- customer portal
- signed file download
- automated document extraction
- full CRM workflow
