# Site Measure Readiness Checklist

Last updated: 17 June 2026

Purpose: help decide when a controlled lead should move from online estimate/review to site measure.

## Recommend Site Measure When

- the customer is close to choosing a provider
- measurements are missing or uncertain
- layout changes are likely
- service relocation may be needed
- apartment/strata access needs review
- older-property demolition or hazardous-material risk may exist
- benchtop/splashback details are unclear
- appliance sizes or specifications are not confirmed
- quote exclusions could materially change scope
- written scope cannot be confirmed from supplied documents

## Prepare Before Measure

Ask customer to prepare:

- existing quote or scope document
- photos of current kitchen
- rough dimensions or plan if available
- appliance list or preferred models
- benchtop/splashback preference
- strata/access notes
- known service relocation questions
- timing and budget band

## Safe Positioning

Use:

- site measure helps confirm project-specific details
- written scope confirmation required
- licensed trades may need to review plumbing, electrical or gas
- apartment/strata works may require approval
- older properties may require asbestos or hazardous-material review

Avoid:

- final pricing guaranteed
- legal approval
- compliance approval
- HBC approval
- strata approval
- instant ordering
