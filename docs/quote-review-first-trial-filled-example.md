# Quote Review First Trial Filled Example

Last updated: 23 June 2026

Purpose: fake no-customer-data example showing how the first unpaid manual quote-review trial should be filled before using a real controlled lead.

Deployment status: not needed. This is a local training document only.

Do not present this as a completed customer job, customer proof, legal review, compliance approval or final quote.

## Example Lead Context

- Lead name: Trusted Tester Example
- Suburb: Neutral Bay
- Property type: Strata apartment
- Project stage: Comparing kitchen quotes
- Current quote available: Yes
- Photos/plans available: Photos only
- Preferred next step: Quote review
- Source: controlled tester
- Message: We have a quote for a small apartment kitchen and want to know whether the inclusions are clear before accepting it.

Customer-safe input summary:

The tester has a kitchen quote and photos. The message suggests an apartment setting, likely access coordination, and uncertainty around whether the quoted total includes demolition, rubbish removal, appliance allowances, splashback and service relocation.

## Review Readiness

Label: `Basic review ready`

Reason:

- A written quote exists.
- Photos and project context exist.
- The lead gives a clear quote-review reason.
- The report cannot become `Strong review ready` until plans, measurements, appliance list, finish selections and detailed quote line items are available.

What would improve confidence:

- quote page screenshots or line-item inclusions
- measured layout or plan
- appliance schedule
- strata/access notes
- explicit benchtop and splashback allowance detail

## Scope Clarity

What appears included:

- cabinetry supply or installation is likely included
- benchtop allowance is likely included
- general kitchen installation is likely included

What appears unclear:

- demolition
- rubbish removal
- splashback supply and installation
- appliance supply, owner-supplied appliances or PC sums
- electrical relocation
- plumbing relocation or reconnection
- delivery, lift booking, parking and strata work-hour assumptions

Written clarification needed:

Ask the provider to confirm each unclear scope item in writing before comparing headline totals.

## Allowance Risk

Allowance risk label: `High`

Reason:

- appliance allowance is not clear
- benchtop detail is not clear
- splashback is not confirmed
- trade relocation is not confirmed
- strata/access assumptions are not confirmed

PC sums and provisional sums:

- Ask whether appliances, tapware, sink, handles, benchtop cut-outs or splashback are fixed inclusions, PC sums or excluded items.
- Ask whether electrical, plumbing, access or rubbish-removal items are provisional sums or subject to site conditions.

## Missing Information

Missing or unclear:

- measured kitchen layout
- cabinet count and layout changes
- appliance list and supply responsibility
- benchtop material, joins, cut-outs and edge detail
- splashback scope
- demolition and rubbish removal
- electrical/plumbing/gas relocation
- strata approval-review requirements
- parking, lift booking and work hours
- final site measure timing

## Customer-Ready Questions

1. Please confirm whether demolition is included in the quoted total.
2. Please confirm whether rubbish removal and disposal are included.
3. Are appliances included, owner-supplied or treated as PC sums?
4. Is electrical relocation included, excluded or provisional?
5. Is plumbing relocation or reconnection included, excluded or provisional?
6. Is splashback supply and installation included?
7. Are benchtop cut-outs, joins, edge details and installation conditions included?
8. Are delivery, parking, lift booking and strata work-hour limits allowed for?
9. Are deposit, written contract and HBC review prompts clear for the likely project value?
10. Is final site measure required before written scope confirmation?

## General Compliance And Risk Prompts

These are general review prompts only and are not legal advice, compliance approval or building certification.

- Written contract review may be required for residential building work over $5,000 including GST.
- Deposit guidance should be reviewed, including 10% maximum deposit guidance.
- HBC review may be required for projects over $20,000 including GST.
- Licensed plumbing, electrical and gas trades require review where those works are included.
- Strata apartment work may require owners corporation review, access coordination, lift booking, parking planning and work-hour confirmation.
- DBP/class 2 screening may be needed for relevant apartment work.
- Engineered-stone restrictions may affect benchtop or splashback options.
- Older properties may require asbestos or hazardous-material review.
- Final site measure is required before project-specific pricing and written scope confirmation.

If the customer wants to continue with Operon Kitchens, request professional review or prepare for site measure after the missing scope items are clearer.

## Internal Trial Lessons

Use this example to check whether a real lead has enough information for a useful unpaid manual trial.

Likely public-form improvements to consider after several trials:

- ask for appliance supply responsibility more directly
- ask whether the project is strata/apartment earlier
- ask whether demolition/rubbish removal is already shown in the quote
- ask whether the quote mentions PC sums or provisional sums
- ask whether the customer has measurements or photos ready

Do not include supplier costs, internal rates, margin, markup logic, lead score, admin priority, internal notes, service keys or hidden pricing logic in customer-facing reports.
